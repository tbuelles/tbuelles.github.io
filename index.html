About

I'm a Research Scientist at Pythia Labs. I work on probabilistic methods in machine learning focusing on (discrete) diffusion models and continuous-time Markov chains. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar in Mathematics at Caltech working with Prof. Graber on Algebraic Geometry and String Theory. I received my PhD in Mathematics from ETH Zürich, advised by Prof. Pandharipande. My thesis concerns the derived categories and enumerative geometry of Calabi–Yau manifolds. Before that, I did my MSc and BSc in Mathematics at the University of Bonn where I worked with Prof. Huybrechts on algebraic cycles and motives of K3 surfaces.

Research

My research is published in Journal of Algebraic Geometry, Forum of Mathematics, and Manuscripta Mathematica.

Teaching

From 2016 to 2023, I was an instructor for Mathematics at the University of Bonn, ETH Zürich, and Caltech, teaching courses in Linear Algebra, Commutative Algebra, Group Theory, Galois Theory, and Algebraic Geometry.
