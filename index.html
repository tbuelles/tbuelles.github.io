About

  • I'm a research scientist at Pythia Labs. I was trained as a mathematican, now I train neural nets. More technically (less funny), I work on generative and probabilistic modeling on structured domains, such as graphs and manifolds.
  • At Pythia Labs, we train generative models on biomolecular data and deploy them to solve problems in protein design.
  • Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Caltech working with Prof. Graber on geometric deep learning and applications in string theory, see here for a summary.
  • I did my PhD in Mathematics at ETH Zurich in the group of Prof. Pandharipande. My thesis focuses on special symmetries of enumerative invariants derived from Calabi-Yau geometries in superstring theory.
  • Before that, I did my BSc and MSc in Mathematics at the University of Bonn where I worked with Prof. Huybrechts on algebraic cycles and motives of K3 surfaces.
