About

  • I'm a research scientist at Pythia Labs. I was trained as a mathematican, now I train neural nets. More technically (less funny), my research focuses on generative and probabilistic modeling of graph structured data on geometric domains.
  • At Pythia Labs, I work on problems in protein design, using multimodal generative models for biomolecular data (e.g. protein sequence and structure)
  • Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Caltech working with Prof. Graber on geometric deep learning and applications in string theory, see here for a summary.
  • I did my PhD in Mathematics at ETH Zurich in the group of Prof. Pandharipande. My thesis focuses on special symmetries of enumerative invariants derived from Calabi-Yau geometries in superstring theory.
  • Before that, I did my BSc and MSc in Mathematics at the University of Bonn where I worked with Prof. Huybrechts on algebraic cycles and motives of K3 surfaces.
  • In academia, I had the chance to teach a number of seminars and graduate courses as instructor for mathematics at Caltech, ETH Zurich, and University of Bonn.
